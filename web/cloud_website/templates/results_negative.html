Though this test isn't definitive, it acts as the first step in understanding if you could be at risk of heart disease. The most important thing to do now is to maintain a balanced and healhty lifestyle and to be aware of potential risks associated with heart disease.

To help you out, we've included some information below which you might find useful.

What is CAD?

Coronary Artery Disease (CAD) is a type of heart disease that has been a leading cause of death in several countries.

The purpose of our coronary arteries is to supply our heart muscle with oxygen-rich blood and CAD is caused when coronary arteries are narrowed down or blocked, resulting in the heart not getting enough oxygen-rich blood.

Quick facts on CAD

    What causes CAD and what are the risk factors?

  • Physically inactive
  • Overweight or obese
  • High blood pressure
  • High cholesterole

    What are the symptoms of CAD?

  • Chest / arm pain or discomfort (Angina)
  • Light-headedness and nausea
  • Cold sweat
  • Shortness of breath

    Complications

  • Chest pain
  • Heart attack
  • Abnormal heart rythm
  • Heart failure

    How to prevent heart diease?

  • Control blood pressure and cholesterol
  • Have a healthy diet
  • Exercise regularly
  • Reduce and manage stress
